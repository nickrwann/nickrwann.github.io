About Me

I was born and raised in Tomball, TX – just 30 minutes north of Houston with my older brother Matthew. My parents are originally from Green Bay, Wisconsin, but moved to Houston for my mother’s medical residency – beginning my father’s career at Compaq/HP as an electrical engineer.

I grew up playing soccer and piano, participating in Boy Scouts, video gaming, and essentially “messing around” with computers – building my first desktop computer in 8th grade. Naturally, I had always assumed that I would grow up to be an engineer, but that certainty soon wavered.

Freshman and sophomore year of high school, I played goalie on my school’s soccer team, ran cross country, and sang in choir – inspiring my dive into the arts. My sophomore year, I joined theatre, competitive one-act play, speech and debate, and chamber choir. After experiencing success in competitive acting and choir - placing 16th in the nation for duet acting at NIETOC and 8th tenor in the state at TPSMEA , I decided to seriously pursue the arts as a career. This prompted me to training at Carnegie Mellon and the Gately/Poole Conservatory in Chicago during my summer and winter breaks of 2016. Split between my two longtime passions, when college decisions came, I was between acting at the University of California Irvine or engineering at Texas A&M. As you may have assumed, I ended up choosing engineering.

While I do still love acting, I have many other passions which I have been able to pursue in college. Outside of school, if I’m not working at HEB, I love to sing, play piano and guitar, shoot photography, play soccer, serve the community, go camping, and travel; I plan to study abroad in Spain this summer!

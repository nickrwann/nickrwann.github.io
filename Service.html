Brotherhood of Aggie Mentors

(BAM)

BAM is a men’s organization on the campus of Texas A&M University which serves the Bryan and College Station community through mentorship by developing leaders of character who uphold the core values of Texas A&M. Mentorship as simply actively seeking to invest in another person.

We believe that mentorship can take place nearly anywhere; a game of ultimate frisbee, a study hall, or even over a shared meal. Bryan High School is our primary source of outreach, meaning that we host events directly geared towards these 9-12th grade students. During the week, we TA in the school and attend school lunches and sports games. On the weekends, we hosts events like pickup basketball, football, and ultimate frisbee tournaments.

However, we affirm and support successful mentorship relationships that occur outside of Bryan H.S. as well.

Impact

Impact Retreat is a 4-day retreat designed to connect freshman to churches and ministries that will provide lasting community on the foundation of Christ and the Gospel. Essentially, Impact is a Christian Fish Camp for freshman.

In the semester prior to retreat, I interviewed and then trained to be a counselor. This consisted of weekly 2 hours training meetings, 2 weekend retreats, and other more spontaneous hangouts in order to get to know our fellow counselors. We were then split into guy/girl partnerships, creating a family-like atmosphere for the freshman.

The week before retreat, all the counselors arrived in college station and prepared to welcome the freshman. When the freshman arrived, each partnership got a “family” which served throughout the four days counseling, entertaining, and welcoming to the University!

Physics Festival

Every spring semester, the Texas A&M Physics department holds a free physics festival where people of all ages can find funny, puzzling, and fascinating hands-on experiments. There are also public lectures held by prominent scientists and astronauts!

I served as a student volunteer, setting up the experiments the week prior and then hosting them throughout the festival. My freshman year, I oversaw the Lenz’ Law Pendulum – describing and demonstrating how eddy currents work. I was upgraded to Tesla Coil the next year, where I helped usher in the crowd and give a simplified description of what a tesla coil is!

Soul in the City

Soul in the City is a week of Christian service, study, worship, food, fun and fellowship. During the week, all participants are housed at St. Timothy’s Anglican Church and serve the city of Houston through different acts of service such as painting houses and working at food banks, non-profit organizations, and soup kitchens. I’ve served as a student participant in 6th-12th grade and afterwards as leader and photographer.
